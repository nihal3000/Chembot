Calculate the standard enthalpy of hydrolysis for the following reaction: 
AlCl3 + 3H2O → Al(OH)3 + 3HCl 
Given that the standard enthalpies of formation for AlCl3(-696.5 kJ/mol), Al(OH)3(-1277.1 kJ/mol), and HCl(-92.3 kJ/mol) are known.
To calculate the standard enthalpy of hydrolysis for the given reaction, we can use the following equation:

ΔH_reaction = Σ ΔH_f(products) - Σ ΔH_f(reactants)

where ΔH_reaction is the standard enthalpy of hydrolysis, ΔH_f(products) are the standard enthalpies of formation for the products, and ΔH_f(reactants) are the standard enthalpies of formation for the reactants.

For the given reaction:

AlCl3 + 3H2O → Al(OH)3 + 3HCl

The standard enthalpies of formation are:

ΔH_f(AlCl3) = -696.5 kJ/mol
ΔH_f(Al(OH)3) = -1277.1 kJ/mol
ΔH_f(HCl) = -92.3 kJ/mol

Now, we can calculate the standard enthalpy of hydrolysis:

ΔH_reaction = [1 × ΔH_f(Al(OH)3) + 3 × ΔH_f(HCl)] - [1 × ΔH_f(AlCl3)]

ΔH_reaction = [1 × (-1277.1 kJ/mol) + 3 × (-92.3 kJ/mol)] - [1 × (-696.5 kJ/mol)]

ΔH_reaction = (-1277.1 kJ/mol - 276.9 kJ/mol) + 696.5 kJ/mol

ΔH_reaction = -857.5 kJ/mol

Therefore, the standard enthalpy of hydrolysis for the given reaction is -857.5 kJ/mol.
