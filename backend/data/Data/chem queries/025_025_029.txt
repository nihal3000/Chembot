3. Collaboration with schools and universities: Work with educational institutions to incorporate more chemistry-related topics into their curricula, emphasizing the practical applications of chemistry in society and the economy.

4. Industry partnerships: Partner with companies and organizations in the chemical industry to showcase their work and demonstrate how chemistry contributes to economic growth and societal well-being.

5. Policy advocacy: Engage with policymakers to emphasize the importance of chemistry in addressing global challenges, such as climate change, food security, and public health. Provide expert advice and recommendations on how chemistry can be integrated into policy decisions.

6. Public events and exhibitions: Organize and participate in public events, such as science fairs, exhibitions, and conferences, to showcase the role of chemistry in society and the economy. Use these events as opportunities to engage with the public and answer their questions about chemistry.

7. Role models and ambassadors: Encourage prominent chemists and chemistry-related professionals to act as role models and ambassadors for the field. These individuals can share their experiences and knowledge with the public, inspiring interest in chemistry and its applications.

8. Online resources: Develop and maintain user-friendly online resources, such as websites, blogs, and social media accounts, that provide accessible information about the role of chemistry in society and the economy.

9. Awards and recognition: Establish awards and recognition programs that celebrate the achievements of chemists and chemistry-related professionals, highlighting their contributions to society and the economy.

10. Continuous dialogue: Maintain an ongoing dialogue with the general public and policymakers about the importance of chemistry in society and the economy. This can be achieved through regular communication, updates, and feedback on chemistry-related issues and developments.
