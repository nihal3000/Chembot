θ = (P * K) / (1 + P * K)

where θ is the fractional coverage of the surface, P is the equilibrium pressure of nitrogen, and K is the Langmuir adsorption constant.

The Clausius-Clapeyron equation is given by:

ln(K) = (-ΔH_ads / R) * (1/T) + C

n = (8.5 x 10^-3 atm) * (0.001 m^3) / (8.314 J/mol*K) * (298 K)
